# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises. It includes core project roles as well as supporting and cross-functional roles that contribute to successful project delivery across the complete lifecycle.

---

## Core Project Roles

The following roles form the primary delivery team for OctoAcme projects:

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Supporting and Cross-Functional Roles

The following roles provide critical expertise and accountability across the project lifecycle, ensuring quality, usability, compliance, and continuous improvement.

---

## QA Engineer

### Role Summary
QA Engineers design, execute, and report on test strategies and plans. They work closely with Developers to validate feature implementation, with Product Managers to verify requirements are met, and with Project Managers to surface quality risks and schedule implications.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain comprehensive test plans covering functional, performance, security, and usability testing
- Execute manual and automated tests and document results
- Identify, validate, and track quality issues through resolution
- Collaborate with Developers on test-driven development and debugging
- Report on test coverage metrics and quality dashboards
- Advise on quality risks and release readiness

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Reduce defects that reach production
- Build confidence in release quality through transparent reporting
- Enable fast iteration through clear quality feedback loops

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- Daily test execution status and bug reports
- Release quality gates and readiness assessments with Project Managers
- Retrospective feedback on testing processes and gaps

### Key Collaborations
- **Developers**: Code review for testability; joint debugging of failed tests
- **Product Managers**: Requirements clarification and acceptance criteria validation
- **Project Managers**: Quality metrics and release timeline impact

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers define user experience flows, visual designs, and interaction patterns. They collaborate with Product Managers to translate requirements into usable designs and with Developers to ensure designs are implemented as intended. They drive accessibility and usability throughout the project.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define interaction patterns and design systems for consistency
- Collaborate with Developers to document designs and component specifications
- Ensure accessibility standards (WCAG) are met
- Iterate on designs based on feedback and user testing
- Review implementations to ensure fidelity to design

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support costs through good design
- Establish design consistency across products and releases
- Drive user satisfaction and retention metrics

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and key stakeholders
- Design specifications and handoff documents for Developers
- Usability testing results and design iteration updates
- Accessibility audits and recommendations

### Key Collaborations
- **Product Managers**: User research insights and feature prioritization alignment
- **Developers**: Technical feasibility discussions and implementation reviews
- **QA Engineers**: Usability and accessibility testing

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers convert complex concepts, decisions, and requirements into clear, accessible documentation. They work with all roles to maintain living documentation in the repository, update process artifacts after releases, and clarify processes for new contributors and stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain process guides, architecture documentation, and runbooks
- Document decisions, trade-offs, and design rationale
- Update artifact templates and checklists based on lessons learned
- Clarify acceptance criteria and requirements where ambiguity exists
- Conduct documentation audits for accuracy and currency
- Support onboarding by maintaining contributor guides
- Review documentation in PRs and code for clarity

### Goals
- Reduce knowledge silos and onboarding time
- Ensure decisions and processes are captured for future reference
- Enable faster problem-solving through accessible documentation
- Support scalability through clear, repeatable processes

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with Project Managers and process owners
- Feedback from Developers and other roles on documentation gaps
- Process updates after retrospectives and major releases
- Contributor feedback through support channels

### Key Collaborations
- **Project Managers**: Process documentation and decision capture
- **All Roles**: Subject matter expertise and review feedback
- **Developers**: Architecture and technical documentation

---

## Customer Support Representative

### Role Summary
Customer Support Representatives surface customer feedback, support tickets, trends, and escalations to Product and Project teams. They help prioritize improvements based on real-world user impact and customer pain points, ensuring the team remains focused on delivering customer value.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze support tickets and customer feedback for trends and patterns
- Escalate critical issues and feature requests to Product/Project teams
- Provide quantitative data on customer pain points and feature impact
- Participate in retrospectives to share customer perspective
- Support release communication to customers
- Contribute to troubleshooting and documentation improvements based on customer issues

### Goals
- Ensure customer voice is heard in prioritization decisions
- Reduce time to resolution for customer issues
- Identify high-impact improvements that drive customer satisfaction
- Bridge the gap between product/engineering teams and real-world user needs

### Typical Communication
- Weekly customer trend reports shared with Product and Project teams
- Issue escalations and urgent incident communication
- Feature request summaries and impact assessments
- Post-release feedback collection and sharing

### Key Collaborations
- **Product Managers**: Customer insights and prioritization impact
- **Project Managers**: Escalation communication and issue tracking
- **Developers**: Technical troubleshooting and bug prioritization

---

## Security/Compliance Lead

### Role Summary
Security/Compliance Leads review architectural decisions, releases, and code for security risks and compliance concerns. They work with Developers and Project Managers to ensure regulatory alignment, manage security incidents, and embed security practices throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct security reviews of architecture, code, and infrastructure changes
- Maintain compliance with applicable regulations and standards
- Identify and assess security risks and propose mitigations
- Coordinate security incident response and communication
- Define and enforce security best practices and policies
- Participate in planning to account for security requirements
- Maintain audit logs and compliance documentation

### Goals
- Prevent security breaches and data loss
- Maintain compliance with regulatory requirements
- Build customer trust through transparent security practices
- Enable fast, secure deployment through embedded security reviews

### Typical Communication
- Security review feedback on PRs and design documents
- Risk assessments and remediation plans with Project Managers
- Compliance audit reports and regulatory updates
- Security incident communications and post-mortems

### Key Collaborations
- **Developers**: Security code review and best practices guidance
- **Project Managers**: Risk assessment and compliance timeline impact
- **Product Managers**: Security requirements for new features
